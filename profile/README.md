Hegel is a universal property-based testing protocol and family of libraries, built on [Hypothesis](https://github.com/HypothesisWorks/hypothesis).

Hegel is primarily maintained by Hypothesis developers, as part of their work at [Antithesis](https://antithesis.com/)[^1]. It is our attempt to bring the quality of property-based testing found in Hypothesis to every language. While Hegel will eventually have superpowers inside of Antithesis, Hegel is designed first and foremost to be a family of high-quality property-based testing libraries on its own terms.

Our current Hegel libraries are:
* [hegel-rust](https://github.com/hegeldev/hegel-rust)
* [hegel-go](https://github.com/hegeldev/hegel-go)

We also have libraries in various states of completion for C++, OCaml, and TypeScript, and expect to release some of them soon.

If you want to learn more, [check out the website](https://hegel.dev/).

[^1]: Hence the name Hegel. It's a philosophy joke.
